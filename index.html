Abstract

We present Robotics Diffusion Transformer with 1.2B parameters (RDT-1B), which is the largest diffusion-based foundation model for robot manipulation, pre-trained on the largest collection of multi-robot datasets with 1M+ episodes. We have collected 6K+ episodes by ourselves via Mobile ALOHA teleoperation to fine-tune RDT and have deployed it on the Mobile ALOHA robot. It has set a new benchmark of generalizability in bimanual manipulation. It supports control of almost all modern manipulators (including bimanual, joints, EEFs, and even chassis) and is ready for the community to fine-tune with their robots🚀!

Model Architecture

Heterogeneous action spaces of various robots are embedded into a unified action space for cross-robot model training. Inputs: low-dimensional proprioception zt, noisy action chunk ãt:t+Ta, control frequency c, and diffusion timestep k, acting as denoising inputs, and image and language inputs, acting as conditions. Outputs: denoised action chunk at:t+Ta.
